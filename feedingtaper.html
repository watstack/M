Formula Transition Plan

Transition feeds outside 9–5 only · You bottle feed, partner pumps simultaneously · Starting 10 April 2026

How this works

→ Daytime (9–5): Completely untouched. Partner breastfeeds solo as now. No changes, no stress.
→ Outside work hours: This is where the transition happens. You give the bottle, partner pumps at the same time.
→ Current outside-work feeds: ~2 am, ~5 am, ~8 am (before you leave), ~6–7 pm (when you're home), ~10 pm (already formula).
→ Pump strategy: Pump replaces the breast feed being dropped. Duration shrinks each phase so supply falls gradually.

Rules to keep supply declining safely

→ One feed swap every 3–4 days minimum. Don't rush phases.
→ Pump only simultaneously with feeds. Never pump in isolation during transition — it maintains supply.
→ Pump to comfort, never to empty. Emptying is a "make more" signal.
→ Daytime stays untouched until Phase 5. Partner has no extra pressure.
→ If engorged between sessions: cold compress first; 3–4 min relief pump only if needed.
→ Red patches + heat + fever = mastitis. Contact GP or midwife same day.
→ Flex is built in. Hold any phase an extra 2–3 days if supply is dropping too fast.
→ Growth spurts: increase formula volume, not breast feeds.
